TT_Coach's core product positioning: **don't re-teach players from scratch, help them improve on top of their existing technique**.

**Why:** Table tennis has no universal "correct" technique. Chinese vs European schools, shakehand vs penhold, classical vs modern topspin — elbow angles, shoulder positions, wrist action differ. A rigid universal rule ("elbow should be 120°") shipped to everyone makes ~half of players feel the app is teaching them wrong — breaks credibility on first session. Every player has technique shaped by their coach; the app should respect that.

**How to apply:**
- Prefer **calibration / personal baseline** over universal rules. Player records 10-20 "reference" strokes; app derives their personal thresholds; rules check *consistency with their own baseline*, not a universal ideal.
- Feedback framing: "your elbow is 15° lower than your own stable baseline" — not "your elbow should be 120°".
- Drill rules are **~5-7 universal meta-rules** (stability, consistency, rhythm deviation from baseline), not technique-specific thresholds.
- This replaces user-authored drill DSL as the Stage 1 core — most amateurs can't articulate their own joint angles, but they can do 10 good reps and let the app derive the baseline.
- Coach-authored drills / authoring UI is a later concern (Stage 2+ or coach persona), not Stage 1.
